# One-page summary

Used at Stage 8 for external critique. Must fit on a single printed page (approximately 500 words).

## Structure

**Title.** One line. No jargon.

**The claim.** Two sentences. What you assert, in plain language.

**Why it matters.** Two sentences. Who benefits if it works, and in what way.

**The evidence so far.** Three sentences. What theoretical and preliminary computational results support the claim. Cite one or two specific results.

**The test.** Two sentences. The experimental or computational measurement that will either confirm or falsify the claim, and the pass/fail criterion.

**The risks.** Three sentences. The top three ways this could fail, ordered by probability.

**The ask.** One sentence. What you want from the reader (funding, collaboration, feedback, permission to proceed).

## Formatting constraints

- 11-point font, single-spaced, 1-inch margins
- No figures unless one figure is the core contribution (then one figure only)
- No bullet lists; prose paragraphs
- No references section; cite inline with author-year

## What belongs in a one-pager vs. a full proposal

Belongs: the claim, the test, the risks, the ask.

Does not belong: derivations, methods, team, budget tables, preliminary figures unless absolutely essential.

If the reader wants more, they will ask. The one-pager exists to get that ask, not to preempt it.
