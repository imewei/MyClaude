# Heilmeier catechism

Answer each question in plain language. No jargon that needs a definition. If you cannot answer a question, the project is not ready for this stage.

---

## 1. What are you trying to do?

Articulate your objective using absolutely no jargon.

Target: 2-4 sentences. A smart person outside your field must be able to read this and know what the work is about.

> [Answer here]

---

## 2. How is it done today, and what are the limits of current practice?

What is the state of the field before your work? What cannot be done or what goes wrong with current methods?

Target: one paragraph. Cite 1-3 specific papers or methods by name.

> [Answer here]

---

## 3. What is new in your approach and why do you think it will be successful?

What specifically is different? Why will it work where others have not?

Target: one paragraph. Be specific. Avoid generic phrases about novelty.

> [Answer here]

---

## 4. Who cares? If you succeed, what difference will it make?

Who uses the result? What changes downstream because this worked?

Target: 2-3 sentences per stakeholder group. Name the groups.

> [Answer here]

---

## 5. What are the risks?

What could go wrong? Order by severity. Each risk should have a probability estimate (low/medium/high) and a mitigation strategy.

Target: 3-5 risks listed. Do not understate; a Heilmeier answer with no real risks is not credible.

> [Answer here]

---

## 6. How much will it cost? How long will it take?

Estimate resources (people, instrument time, compute, funding) and timeline. If the cost depends on decisions not yet made, list the decisions.

Target: numerical estimates with explicit assumptions.

> [Answer here]

---

## 7. What are the midterm and final exams to check for success?

What measurement or result, at what time, will tell you the work is succeeding or failing? Midterm exams are the early signals; final exam is the success criterion for the whole project.

Target: at least two midterm exams and one final exam, each with a quantitative pass/fail criterion.

> [Answer here]

---

## Self-check before submission

- Does question 1 avoid all jargon? If not, rewrite.
- Does question 3 specify what is different rather than gesturing? If not, rewrite.
- Does question 5 name risks you would actually lose sleep over, not just pro forma ones? If not, rewrite.
- Does question 7 have a quantitative pass/fail, not a subjective one? If not, rewrite.
