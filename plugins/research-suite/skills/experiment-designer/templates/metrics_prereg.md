# Pre-registered success metrics

Fixed at Stage 7. Not revised post-hoc. The metrics turn the Stage 3 claim into a binary pass/fail that the Stage 6 predicted observable and the Stage 7 measurement can resolve.

## For each claim component

```
### Metric N: [name]

**What is measured:** [observable, reference to Stage 6 predicted observable file]

**Sample size:** [N, from power analysis]

**Test to be used:** [specific statistical test, with alpha and expected power]

**PASS threshold:** [quantitative; e.g., "spectral gap drop exceeds 1.0 (dimensionless) within 30 s of G'/G'' crossover in at least 80% of replicates"]

**FAIL threshold:** [if different from simple negation of PASS; e.g., "no gap drop exceeding 0.2 within 60 s in any replicate"]

**INCONCLUSIVE region:** [what lies between; what the plan does if the measurement lands here]

**Pre-registered on:** [date]
```

## Why the inconclusive region matters

Most measurements do not cleanly pass or fail. A well-designed metric names the inconclusive region explicitly and specifies what happens if the data lands there:

- Run additional replicates at the original conditions
- Extend the measurement to a different regime
- Declare the result ambiguous and pursue a different line of evidence
- Accept the result as null and revise the claim

Whatever the plan says, it is committed to before data collection.

## What counts as a valid pre-registration

- The metric is stated as a specific numeric threshold, not a direction
- The test, sample size, alpha, and power are named
- The interpretation of each outcome (pass, fail, inconclusive) is written down
- The document is versioned with a date, and the date precedes data collection

## What pre-registration does not cover

- Exploratory analyses (welcome, but labeled as exploratory in the paper)
- Post-hoc hypotheses generated by the pre-registered measurement (fine to generate, require their own confirmatory study)
- Checks for data quality (pre-specify the quality criteria too, but these are separate from the primary metrics)
